Alestur Wolf

Species: Wood Elf
Age: 28 yrs
Class: Gloom Stalker ranger
Story: Abandoned by his unknown parents and raised by the harsh lessons of the streets of Vladrich, he grew to be a cunning master of the city's urban districts. Surviving by his experience and quick hands, he learned one thing: no one was on his side. That was until he met Alexandria, a high-elf he found begging in the streets of Elara's Capitol. She was the first step to him learning that there is more to life than surviving it.

Alexandria Willthorne

Species: High-Elf
Age: 32 yrs
Class: Way of Mercy Monk
Story: A high elf found in low places, Alexandria found herself struggling to survive after her parents were killed because of a feud she did not know existed. After years of travel and struggle, she found herself in Elara's Capitol, wishing her parents had coddled her less and taught her how to survive. As she stopped for the night and tried to rest, a few members of an Elarian patrol found themselves bored and began to harass and beat her. Thankfully, Alestur was nearby and had plenty of untreated trauma to take out on them.

Grogth

Species: Half-Orc
Age: 42 yrs
Class: Path of the Ancestral Guardian Barbarian
Story: Grogth spent the last decade wandering Elara and Wustoria, trying to find a way to honor his ancestors. His mother had fallen in love with a human, a union his clan could not tolerate. He grew up an outcast among his kin, all of whom were famous for their conquests, performed to please their ancestors and pass on tradition. One day, he saw a dispute break out between residents and the guards of an Elarian outpost. The conflict ended as all do when a militant force is involved: in blood, tears, and corpses. Grogth noticed two children trapped between bodies and blades and rushed to protect them. Suddenly, an arrow flew from nowhere and struck a guard about to attack Grogth from behind. Landing squarely in the only uncovered part of his neck, the guard fell. Then, as if materializing, two elves leapt in: Alestur and Alexandria. Alexandria grabbed the children and took them to cover while Alestur finished off the remaining guards. They then aided whoever they could, giving supplies and healing while taking nothing in return. In that moment, something stirred in Grogth. He decided that by serving the meek, he would also be serving his ancestors. At first, Alestur refused to let Grogth join them. "Orcs are just humans with more anger and less intellect," Alestur said. "Both only strive for power at the expense of anything, including their own kind." Grogth learned that Alestur actively hated and hunted humans and orcs, but only those he witnessed harming the weak and innocent. After Grogth explained his desires, Alestur gave him a chance. The orc went from a possible enemy to a brother Alestur never thought he would have.

Wilks Adams

Species: Human
Age: 16 yrs
Class: Undescovered
Story: Struggles between those with power and those without. What's new? All that mattered to me was my sister, Seriah, and I would do anything to protect her. But I couldn't do much, and no one ever helped. Since no one ever helped, I never thought to ask. In fact, I refused. I figured no one would help even if I did, and I didn't have time to find out. I will never forget that day, though. All three of them—the elf couple and the orc—didn't even know each other. Yet they threw themselves into danger without hesitation to protect strangers, to protect my sister and me. I had never owed anything to anyone before, but if I had to owe my life to someone, I'm glad it was them./

Seriah Adams

Species: Human
Age: 13 yrs
Class: Undescovered
Story: My brother always held my hand firmly, his grip a constant, protective pressure. He was always scanning our surroundings for threats; for the "monsters" he warned me about. I rarely knew what he meant. He’d always remind me that in this world, it was just the two of us against everything else, so I never strayed far from his side. One time, some aggressive men started shouting at us, closing in. In an instant, my brother pulled me behind him, whispering fiercely for me to stay quiet. I was terrified and squeezed my eyes shut. When I finally dared to open them, the scene had changed completely. An elf woman with ears like sharpened leaves stood there, beside a man with intense, piercing eyes. And with them, an orc. He was a mountain of green muscle. All he did was let out a low, rumbling growl, and the men who had been so threatening just seconds before scattered. The elf woman offered me a gentle smile, and the fear just melted away. But it was my brother’s reaction that I remember most. His mouth was hanging open, his face a mask of pure disbelief. He had drilled it into my head my whole life that no one would ever help us, that we were on our own. And yet, these strangers had. From that day on, we became a strange kind of family. And though we were all supposedly brothers and sisters, it was clear that Alestur and Alexandria had become the parents we never had.
